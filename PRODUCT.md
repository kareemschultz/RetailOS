# Product

## Register

product

## Users

RetailOS serves cashiers, store managers, warehouse workers, accountants, business owners, executives, MSP/support operators, and later storefront customers. They work in retail, wholesale, warehousing, bonded-warehouse, logistics, and multi-branch business contexts where uptime, auditability, inventory accuracy, and low training burden matter.

## Product Purpose

RetailOS is an offline-first, multi-tenant retail ERP and POS platform. It unifies POS, inventory, warehousing, bonded goods, accounting, CRM, procurement, ecommerce, reporting, SaaS administration, and white-label deployment over one authoritative backend. Success means a business can keep selling and operating during connectivity failures while management still gets trustworthy stock, sales, financial, and audit data.

## Brand Personality

Calm, capable, exacting. The product should feel like a modern enterprise operating system: polished enough for executives and accountants to trust, direct enough for cashiers and warehouse teams to use under pressure, and flexible enough for many verticals without looking generic.

## Anti-references

Do not resemble AdminLTE, Bootstrap dashboards, legacy SAP GUI, old Odoo themes, legacy Microsoft Dynamics screens, or generic Tailwind CRUD. Avoid decorative dashboards, marketing-style card grids, emoji UI, unclear icons, client-side business calculations, and any interface that hides operational status or financial consequences.

## Design Principles

Backend truth, frontend clarity: the UI renders backend DTOs and availability decisions; it does not compute business facts.

Dense but humane: tables, metrics, and operational workflows must be scannable without feeling cramped.

Role-aware complexity: show cashiers, warehouse staff, managers, accountants, and owners the actions and depth they need, then progressively disclose the rest.

Offline confidence: sync state and risk must be visible so users understand whether work is saved, queued, or blocked.

Owned composition: imported AdminCN, CommerceO, Studio, or shadcn UI becomes RetailOS-owned code, re-themed and wired to the RetailOS backend.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

RetailOS targets WCAG 2.2 AA. Interfaces need keyboard navigation, visible focus, color-plus-icon status indicators, reduced-motion support, high-contrast text, large enough touch targets for POS and warehouse workflows, and clear validation that explains how to fix problems.
